Neural Network aided Impulsive Perturbation Decoding for Short LDPC Codes

As short packet communications become more important, multi-round belief propagation decoding with impulsive perturbation (MBPD-IP) was proposed to improve the decoding of short low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes.

Author: Hyunjae Lee, Young-Sung Kil, Min Young Chung, Sang-Hyo Kim

Publisher: IEEE Wireless Communications Letters

July 14, 2021

Molecular Communication with Passive Receivers in Anomalous Diffusion Channels

We consider anomalous diffusion for molecular communication with a passive receiver. We first consider the probability density function of molecules’ location at a given time in a space of arbitrary dimension.

Authors: Dung Phuong Trinh, Youngmin Jeong, Sang-Hyo Kim

Publisher: IEEE Wireless Communications Letters

April 2, 2021

Improving the Tradeoff Between Error Correction and Detection of Concatenated Polar Codes

In this paper, we propose a configuration design scheme for the SCL decoder and a post-decoding validation check scheme in order to provide a better tradeoff between error-detection and error-correction performance.

Authors: Min Jang, Joohyun Lee, Sang-Hyo Kim, Kyeongcheol Yang

Publisher: IEEE Transactions on Communications
